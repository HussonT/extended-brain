A pre-assessment may be completed in a number of ways, for example as a self-service web form issued to employees following a town-hall automation introductory session or completed by business analysts as part of a team brainstorming session. As well as basic details on contact information and departmental information (such as the business division and team they are part of), other details to gather could include: A brief explanation of the process to be automated, including key inputs and outputs A list of IT systems the process interacts with Whether detailed procedure-level process documentation exists The likelihood of the process changing and, if so, over what time period The level of standardization of the process What approximate proportion of the process is rules-based, as opposed to decision-making The estimated financial benefits of automating the process, including categories of benefits such as cost reduction and labor-based FTE An estimate of how many people are required to work on the process An estimate of process timings and volumes. Each of the responses provided could then support a scoring model that can be adapted based on the changing needs of the business and the growing intelligent automation capabilities of the enterprise.
